Role: Developer, Brainstormer/Idea Generator Tools: Ruby on Rails, Haml, CSS (Bootstrap), Heroku
<

My group and I built this site for a staff member at UC Berkeley who wanted a way to track other faculty memeber's interest in various project he was working on around campus. We spoke with him every two weeks to iterate on our design, and we then added and tested any features he requested. None of us had every built a site that was functional from the database to the UI, so a major challenge for us was learning how an application works across the entire web stack. The biggest technical challenge was syncing with the Google Calendar, LinkedIn, and Berkeley API's to retrieve data, and then itegrating that data into the site. Assassins Mobile App
Role: Developer, Designer Tools: Android/Toq Watch (Java, XML), FramerJS, Adobe CC
<
>
Assassins is a social game used as an ice breaker in which a facilitator runs/organizes the game and 2 or more players participate. Each player is given a target who they are supposed to "assassinate" in a method determined by the facilitator. An example would be touching your target with a spoon. I worked in a team of 5 to develop this app for Android and the Qualcom Toq Watch. Our main goal was to make the facilitator's job easier while preserving the main components of the game, namely its social component. Role: Developer, Designer Tools: HTML, CSS, Javascript
<

Scrapi is a site I made to work on my front-end and design skills. It is a scrapbooking site that allows people to collect their photos online and make scrapbooks from those collections. I made 3 views for the site: the homepage, a page to view all collections, and a page to view/edit the photos in a collection. In the interest of keeping the project file fairly small, all the photos are loaded from the internet. KiP: IBM Watson App
Role: Designer, Ideation Tools: FramerJS, Adobe CC
<
>
I worked in a group to come up with and design an app that leveraged IBM's Watson. The course was bussiness oriented, so the focus was on making the app economically lucrative. IBM explained to us how Watson worked, and we were given a fresh corpus to work with (essentially a blank slate). The challenge was coming up with an app/business model that trained Watson while also generating revenue. A secondary objective (per the professor's request) was to aid Indian health care. Our app helps students study by allowing them to make flashcards/QA pairs that other students can also view. We would then use those QA pairs to train a Watson corpus. Our revenue would come from hosipitals and medical companies buying the trained corpus to diagnose and treat patients. A demonstration of the prototype is on Youtube.
